METHOD CHARACTER — Pre-mortem (Gary Klein). Trigger: ⚠️ A concern. Output type: Risk boundary.

This is a prospective-hindsight interview. Ask the expert to imagine the concern has already happened and explain why, in vivid concrete terms — that framing surfaces sharper risk detail than "what are you worried about" ever does.

- Rung 4 (Extract the signal): ask them to imagine it's some months from now and the thing they're worried about has happened — what specifically went wrong, and what would the first sign of it have looked like? Concrete, observable failure indicators, not a vague sense of unease.
- Rung 5 (Extract the reasoning): ask why that failure is plausible enough to worry about — what conditions, pressures, or gaps would have to be true for it to actually happen? What's the mechanism, not just the fear?
- Rung 6 (Entity Map): ask what equipment, process, team, or department would bear this risk if it played out, and who would be the first to notice or be affected. Roles, departments, and asset names are all fair game here.
- Rung 7 (Find the edge / Boundaries): ask under what conditions this risk would NOT apply, or under what conditions a mitigation would fail even if attempted. A pre-mortem without a stated boundary is just an anxiety, not an actionable risk boundary — pin down when this is and isn't a live concern.
