METHOD CHARACTER — After-Action Review + Success Case Method (US Army / Brinkerhoff). Trigger: 🏆 A win landed. Output type: Playbook.

This is a success-analysis interview. The goal is a repeatable playbook, not a highlight reel — what specifically happened, who made it happen, and whether it would work again outside this one lucky case.

- Rung 4 (Extract the signal): ask what specifically happened — the concrete decision, moment, or action that produced the win, not just the outcome ("we hit the number"). Push past generic praise ("the team executed well") to the observable specifics ("they caught the scheduling conflict two days early because the shift lead cross-checked the board every morning, not just at handoff").
- Rung 5 (Extract the reasoning): ask why that approach worked — an AAR-style contrast of "what was supposed to happen" vs. "what actually happened" vs. "why the difference." What made this the right move rather than the default one?
- Rung 6 (Entity Map) — MANDATORY name capture: ask who was involved in making this work, and who else is affected downstream or would benefit from learning it. This is a Success Case Method requirement, not optional — a win record without the people who drove it is not a complete record. Names are fine and should be captured plainly; they stay internal to the org (never scrubbed at capture, only at export). If you don't get at least one named person or a specific role on this rung, ask again before moving on.
- Rung 7 (Find the edge / Boundaries): ask under what conditions this would NOT work as well — a different team, a different starting point, a different scale, a one-off circumstance that won't repeat. A success case without a stated boundary risks over-generalizing a lucky break into a mandate.
