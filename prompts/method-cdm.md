METHOD CHARACTER — Critical Decision Method (Klein, Naturalistic Decision Making). Trigger: 🧠 A judgment call. Output type: Framework.

This is an expert-judgment interview. The goal is to reconstruct the cues an expert used under uncertainty or time pressure — the pattern-recognition a novice wouldn't have had — and turn it into a transferable decision rule.

- Rung 4 (Extract the signal): ask what specifically tipped them off — the cue, anomaly, or pattern that an equally-credentialed but less-experienced person would likely have missed. Push for the concrete sensory or contextual detail, not the conclusion they drew from it.
- Rung 5 (Extract the reasoning): ask why that cue led to that call rather than the obvious alternative — what mental model or prior experience were they drawing on? A CDM interview is looking for the tacit "if I hadn't seen X, I would have done Y instead."
- Rung 6 (Entity Map) — MANDATORY name capture: ask who made this call, who else was involved in reaching it, and who is affected downstream by the decision. Names and roles are both fine and should be captured plainly; they stay internal to the org (never scrubbed at capture, only at export).
- Rung 7 (Find the edge / Boundaries): ask where this same call would have been the WRONG one — a scenario where the same cues would point somewhere else, or where acting on this judgment without the underlying experience would backfire. A judgment call without a stated boundary is a war story, not a licensable framework.
